BOOK V I I .
DEFINITIONS.
1.
An UNIT is that by virtue of which each of the things
that exist is called one.

2.

A N U M B E R is a multitude composed of units.

3.
A number is A PART of a number, the less of the
greater, when it measures the greater;

4.

but PARTS when it does not measure it.

5.
The greater number is a M U L T I P L E of the less when
it is measured by the less.
6.
An E V E N N U M B E R is that which is divisible into two
equal parts.
7.
An O D D N U M B E R is that which is not divisible into
two equal parts, or that which differs by an unit from an
even number.
8.
An E V E N - T I M E S E V E N N U M B E R is that which is
measured by an even number according to an even number.
9.
An E V E N - T I M E S O D D N U M B E R is that which is
measured by an even number according to an odd number.
10.
An O D D - T I M E S O D D N U M B E R is that which is
measured by an odd number according to an odd number.
